Botany - Detailed Study

Botany is the scientific study of plants, their structure, growth, classification, and importance. Plants are essential for life because they produce oxygen through photosynthesis and provide food. They also supply medicines, timber, and ecological balance. Plants are classified into two major groups: 1. Flowering plants (Angiosperms) – These plants produce flowers and seeds. 2. Non-flowering plants (Gymnosperms) – These plants produce seeds without flowers. Major plant examples: Mango – fruit tree, provides food Neem – medicinal plant, used for skin diseases Banyan – large tree, provides oxygen Bamboo – fast growing plant, used in construction Rose – flowering plant, used in decoration Wheat – food crop, provides flour Rice – staple food, grows in water Tulsi – medicinal plant, improves immunity Cactus – desert plant, stores water Sunflower – oil producing plant Classification: - Kingdom: Plantae - Division: Angiosperms/Gymnosperms - Uses: Food, medicine, oxygen, environment balance
